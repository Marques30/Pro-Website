Quality Assurance

Quality Assurance is the testing of products during it's stages of development, it can mean the difference between a perfect product or complete failure and cost up to millions to repair. The sole purpose of Quality Assurance is to find bugs within the program, blueprint, or item your company is creating. A bug is basically an error within the product, lack of information or even in the blueprints, a bug is the main thing that triggers the product to fail and collapse. So as a Quality Assurance or QA it is your job to find the bugs and prevent disasters such as collapsed buildings, Y2K, and at times flying sharks.

Although in order to be a QA you must first learn how to test said product. There are many ways to test a product such as reading the code over and over to inspect it or trying it out. But this can easily get annoying, so another way of testing is automation which is a program you create to control the tests and predict their outcomes. But in order to use automation you must first learn programming.
